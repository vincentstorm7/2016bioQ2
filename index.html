Punnet Square

Named after Reginald C. Punnett, the Punnett Square is a diagram used to predict the outcomes of breeding experiments. This is mostly used by biologists to determine the probability of having offspring with a particular genotype. It uses alleles from both parents to examine the probabilities of acquiring certain combinations. The Punnett Square is a visual representation of the modes of inheritance, and can be used for both mono- and multihybrid crosses.
